Initial Stage

At the beginning of the project, I received a complete UI design package from the Project Manager, along with the responsibility to implement both front-end and back-end functionality. The development was required to be carried out using the company’s internal Oracle Web Content Management System (WCMS) framework.

This framework provided a structured environment for managing templates, components, and content, while also introducing specific technical constraints that needed to be considered during development. My initial focus was on understanding how the Oracle WCMS architecture handled content rendering, component integration, and system workflows, ensuring that the UI designs could be accurately translated into functional, maintainable code. This preparation phase was critical to aligning the visual design with the platform’s capabilities and ensuring long-term scalability within the enterprise system.

Middle Stage

During development, I encountered multiple challenges, particularly with layout alignment and implementing functional logic within the WCMS environment. To overcome these issues, I invested significant time in research, testing, and refining my approach. I collaborated closely with my teammates and the Project Manager, discussing problems in real time and resolving issues efficiently. Through continuous communication and iteration, we improved both the technical solution and the overall user experience.

Final Stage

As a result of strong collaboration and problem-solving, we successfully completed the project with high-quality outcomes. All content and functionality were fully implemented, tested, and deployed within the system. The final product met the design requirements, functioned smoothly within the internal framework, and delivered a reliable and polished user experience.
